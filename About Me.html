About Me

My Approach

My counselling training was in an approach called psychosynthesis. Broadly speaking, psychosynthesis views life as a journey of ongoing development. Diffiulties in the here and now can often be traced back to the past - exploring earlier experiences can therefore help to gain understanding and resolution to current problems.
There is also an emphasis on the future i.e. exploring what is emerging and what changes are seeking to take place in a person’s life in order for them to live more closely aligned with deeper values and aspirations. Having experienced an existential crisis of my own some years ago, I am strongly aware of the inner conflict that can occur when we neglect our deeper yearnings.

I often employ mindfulness approaches in my work . Mindfulness is a powerful tool to enable a greater capacity for self-awareness and self-reflection - this can lead to greater resilience and enhanced well-being, and can contribute greatly to the counselling process.


Some of the issues I work with include:

Addiction, Anger management, Anxiety, Depression, Generalised anxiety disorder, Low self-confidence, Obsessive compulsive disorder, Panic disorder, Phobias, Relationship issues, Self-harm, Spirituality issues/ Spiritual emergency, Stress
*Please note, this list is not exhaustive.

I am a qualified member of BACP, the registering body for counselling and psychotherapy in the UK and abide by their code of ethics and values. Further information can be found at: http://www.bacp.co.uk/ethical_framework
